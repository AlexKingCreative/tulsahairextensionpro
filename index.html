Common Questions

Everything You
Need to Know

When applied correctly by a trained artist, extensions should have zero negative impact on your natural hair. Hope begins every appointment with a health assessment — if your hair isn't in the right condition for a particular method, she'll recommend an alternative or a prep protocol first. Improper installation (too tight, wrong method for hair type, or poor aftercare) is the cause of extension damage — not extensions themselves.
It depends on the method. Sew-In Wefts need a move-up every 4–8 weeks and the wefts themselves can be reused for up to a year. Keratin Bonds typically last 3–5 months before removal and reinstall. Hair Tinsel lasts weeks to a few months depending on your care routine. Toppers and Wigs are long-term investments that last years with proper care. Hope will walk you through realistic expectations for your chosen method at your consultation.
Every client leaves with a full personalized aftercare guide. The basics: use sulfate-free shampoo, brush from ends upward with a soft-bristle extension brush, avoid excessive heat at the bonds or weft seams, and sleep with a loose braid or silk pillowcase. Hope recommends specific products at your appointment based on your hair type and the method used — never generic advice.
That's exactly what the consultation is for. The right method depends on your natural hair density, texture, lifestyle, and goals. Fine hair does best with lightweight methods like hand-tied wefts. Active clients often prefer the durability of keratin bonds. Those wanting a low-commitment option love tinsel. Hope never recommends a method before she's assessed your hair — and she'll always give you an honest answer, even if that means telling you to wait.
